Learn to Stay Safe Online

Simple guides on the most common digital threats. Tap any topic to expand.

0 of 4 read

Fake news is deliberately false or misleading information presented as real news. It spreads fast through social media and messaging apps.

Three types:

  • Misinformation — False content shared without bad intent.
  • Disinformation — Deliberately created to deceive people.
  • Malinformation — Real info used out of context to cause harm.

Warning signs:

  • Shocking headlines with all-caps or excessive exclamation marks
  • No author name or suspicious-looking website URL
  • No other news outlet reporting the same story
  • Out-of-context or manipulated images
💡 Tip: Use the SIFT method — Stop, Investigate the source, Find better coverage, Trace the claim.

Phishing is when attackers pretend to be trusted sources — banks, apps, or government — to steal your passwords, OTPs, or card details.

Common types:

  • Email Phishing — Fake emails asking you to "verify" your account.
  • Smishing (SMS) — Fake prize or alert messages with malicious links.
  • Vishing (Call) — Calls pretending to be bank or police officers.
  • Spear Phishing — Targeted attacks using your personal information.

How to protect yourself:

  • Never click links in suspicious SMS or emails
  • Go directly to the website instead of clicking links
  • Legitimate banks will NEVER ask for your OTP
  • Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)
⚠️ Example: "Your SBI account is blocked! Verify at sbi-secure-update.xyz" — this is a phishing attack.

Deepfakes use AI to create realistic but fake videos, images, or audio of real people saying or doing things they never did.

How it works:

  1. AI is trained on thousands of images/videos of a person
  2. It learns to mimic their face, voice, and expressions
  3. It generates realistic fake content that's very hard to spot

How to spot deepfakes:

  • Unnatural blinking or frozen facial expressions
  • Blurry or warped edges around hair and ears
  • Voice doesn't match lip movements precisely
  • Background looks inconsistent or glitchy
💡 Use TruthShield's Image Checker to detect AI-generated or manipulated images.

A few simple habits can protect you from most online threats.

  • 🔐Strong passwords — Use unique passwords for every account. Consider a password manager.
  • 📲Enable 2FA — Two-Factor Authentication adds a second lock even if your password is stolen.
  • 🔍Verify before sharing — Cross-check viral content with trusted sources before forwarding.
  • 🚫Never share OTPs — No bank or government will ever ask for your OTP.
  • 🔄Keep apps updated — Updates fix security vulnerabilities. Don't ignore them.
  • 📡Avoid public Wi-Fi — Use mobile data or a VPN for banking and email.
